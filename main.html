What is Vedanta?

Literally it means the end of Vedas. End in the sense that it gives us the knowledge of the ultimate Reality, the Self, and the means of realizing it, which is the main goal of human existence. 
Four Vedas viz. Rig, Yajur, Sama and Atharva consist of 100,000 Shlokas divided into Karma Kanda: 80,000; Upasana Kanda: 16,000; and the Jnana Kanda: 4,000 Shlokas. It is this Jnana Kanda portion of the Vedas that is called Vedanta and consists of 1180 Upanishads.

Advaita Vedanta, therefore, means that the ultimate Reality also known as Brahman, alone is the Truth and all else is a superimposition upon this Truth, i.e.,

"Brahmm Satyam Jagat Mithya". 

 

Vedanta is not a religion or a dogma, it is the total philosophy of life, the life that is pulsating alike through humans, animals, plant kingdom and the rest of it. This life principle is variously known as the Self, God, Truth, Consciousness etc. and is of the nature of Bliss Absolute. It is none other than I the Self and is my own essential nature. Discover this Truth through Vedanta, Who am I?, and free yourself from the shackles of repeated births and deaths. 

___ Swami Radhamadhavananda Saraswati

Know the One, by knowing That nothing else remains to be known.


God and I in space alone and nobody else in view.
"And where are the people, O Lord," I said,
"the earth below and the sky o'er head
and the dead whom once I knew?"

"That was a dream," God smiled and said,
"A dream that seemed to be true.
There were no people, living or dead,
there was no earth, and no sky o'er head;
there was only Myself -- in you."

"Why do I feel no fear," I asked,
"meeting You here this way?
For I have sinned I know full well --
and is there heaven, and is there hell,
and is this the Judgment Day?"

"Nay, those were but dreams,"
the Great God said,
"Dreams that have ceased to be.
There are no such things as fear or sin;
there is no you -- you never have been --
there is nothing at all but Me."
 

by Ella Wheeler Wilcox
